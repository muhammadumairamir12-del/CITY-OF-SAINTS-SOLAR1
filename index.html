        Dental Knowledge Hub

        Empower yourself with knowledge about common dental conditions, treatments, and preventive measures. A healthy smile starts with understanding.

        🦠
        Dental Caries: Causes & Prevention
        Understanding tooth decay and how to avoid it.
        +

        Dental caries, commonly known as tooth decay, is a disease that causes the destruction of tooth structure. It occurs when bacteria in your mouth produce acids from the sugars in the food and drinks you consume. These acids attack tooth enamel, leading to cavities.

        • **Prevention:**
        • Brushing twice daily with fluoride toothpaste.
        • Reducing sugar intake in your diet.
        • Regular dental check-ups and professional cleanings.
        • Using dental floss daily.
        🔩
        Dental Implants: Advantages & Benefits
        The modern solution for missing teeth.
        +

        Dental implants are titanium screws surgically placed into the jawbone beneath the gum line to replace missing tooth roots. Once integrated with the bone, they provide a strong foundation for artificial teeth like crowns, bridges, or dentures.

        • **Advantages:**
        • **Long-lasting:** Can last 15–25 years or longer with proper care.
        • **Looks like natural teeth:** Blend seamlessly with your existing teeth.
        • **Prevents bone loss:** Stimulate the jawbone, preventing deterioration.
        • **Improved chewing and speech:** Restore full functionality.
        🛠️
        Dental Restorations: Repairing Damaged Teeth
        Common procedures to restore tooth health and function.
        +

        Dental restorations are procedures used to repair damaged teeth, restoring their function, integrity, and morphology. They are essential for treating cavities, fractures, and other forms of tooth damage, preventing further decay or loss.

        • **Examples:**
        • **Fillings:** Used to fill cavities after decay is removed.
        • **Crowns:** Caps placed over damaged teeth to restore shape, strength, and appearance.
        • **Bridges:** Used to replace one or more missing teeth by spanning the gap.
        • **Veneers:** Thin, custom-made shells bonded to the front of teeth for cosmetic improvement.

        Have questions? We've got answers. Browse through our most common patient queries to find the information you need quickly and easily.

        Is root canal treatment painful? +
        Absolutely not! At Al-Fazal Dental, root canal treatment is completely pain-free thanks to our advanced local anesthesia techniques and gentle approach. Most patients are pleasantly surprised at how comfortable the procedure is.
        How long does teeth whitening last? +
        Professional teeth whitening results typically last between 1 to 3 years. For the best long-term outcomes and to maintain your dazzling smile, we recommend touch-up sessions every 6 to 12 months, depending on your lifestyle and dietary habits.
        What is the cost of dental implants? +
        For information on the cost of dental implants at Al-Fazal Dental, please consult with our team. We offer flexible installment payment options to make this advanced treatment accessible. Dental implants can last 15–25 years or longer with proper care.
        What age is best for braces? +
        While braces are highly effective for children and adolescents, typically between ages 10–14, they are equally effective for adults looking to correct misaligned teeth. We also offer discreet clear aligners as an excellent option for adult orthodontics.
        Do you treat dental emergencies? +
        Yes, absolutely! We understand that dental emergencies can happen at any time. Al-Fazal Dental is open daily until 11:30 PM and we welcome all dental emergencies. Please call us immediately at 0321-4105096 for urgent care.

        What causes tooth decay? +

        Are dental fillings painful? +
        No, dental fillings are performed under local anesthesia to ensure a comfortable and pain-free experience. You might feel some pressure, but no sharp pain.
